# Source Notes and Provenance

This skill is an original synthesis. It paraphrases durable concepts and does not reproduce book chapters or paid source material.

## Source priority

1. Current official platform and standards documentation.
2. Current editions of professional literature.
3. Foundational literature for durable principles.
4. Project-specific evidence and tested user behavior.

When a book conflicts with current operating-system behavior, the official platform guidance takes precedence.

## Recent and acclaimed professional literature

### Laws of UX, Second Edition — Jon Yablonski, 2024

Used for: familiarity, target acquisition, choice complexity, memory, salience, perceived usability, peak/end effects, and responsible use of psychology.

Publisher reference: https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/laws-of-ux/9781098146955/

### Just Enough Research, 2024 edition — Erika Hall

Used for: framing uncertainty, choosing proportionate research, evidence quality, ethics, and integrating research into product decisions.

Author/publisher reference: https://www.mulebooks.com/just-enough-research

### Interviewing Users, Second Edition — Steve Portigal, 2023

Used for: interview planning, neutral questioning, listening, remote research, analysis, bias, and research impact.

Publisher reference: https://rosenfeldmedia.com/books/interviewing-users-second-edition/

### Design That Scales — Dan Mall, 2023

Used for: sustainable design systems, repeated solutions, adoption, governance, contribution, and organizational practice.

Publisher reference: https://rosenfeldmedia.com/books/design-that-scales/

### Continuous Discovery Habits — Teresa Torres, 2021

Used for: outcome-oriented discovery, opportunity mapping, comparing solutions, and testing assumptions continuously.

Author reference: https://www.producttalk.org/continuous-discovery-habits/

### Writing Is Designing — Michael J. Metts and Andy Welfle, 2020

Used for: treating interface language as design, collaboration, labels, errors, onboarding, and testing content.

Publisher reference: https://rosenfeldmedia.com/books/writing-is-designing/

### Designing with the Mind in Mind, Third Edition — Jeff Johnson, 2020

Used for: perception, attention, reading, memory, recognition, motor behavior, and the cognitive basis of UI guidelines.

Publisher reference: https://www.elsevier.com/books/designing-with-the-mind-in-mind/johnson/978-0-12-818202-4

### Articulating Design Decisions, Second Edition — Tom Greever, 2020

Used for: connecting design rationale to goals, explaining tradeoffs, handling critique, and making decisions reviewable.

Publisher reference: https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/articulating-design-decisions/9781492079217/

### Designing Interfaces, Third Edition — Jenifer Tidwell, Charles Brewer, and Aynne Valencia, 2019

Used as a supplementary pattern reference for information architecture, navigation, controls, mobile interfaces, and reusable interaction patterns. Current platform guidance overrides dated examples.

Publisher reference: https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/designing-interfaces-3rd/9781492051954/

## Foundational exceptions

### The Design of Everyday Things, revised and expanded — Don Norman, 2013

Used for: conceptual models, discoverability, affordances, signifiers, mapping, feedback, constraints, and human-centered treatment of error.

Author reference: https://jnd.org/books/the-design-of-everyday-things-revised-and-expanded-edition/

### Don't Make Me Think, Revisited — Steve Krug, 2014

Used for: self-evident interfaces, scanability, conventions, concise content, navigation clarity, and lightweight usability testing.

Author reference: https://sensible.com/dont-make-me-think/

### Refactoring UI — Adam Wathan and Steve Schoger, 2018

Used for: developer-friendly visual hierarchy, spacing, typography, color, depth, component refinement, and systematic visual decisions.

Official reference: https://www.refactoringui.com/

## Refresh policy

Review these notes when:

- a major iOS, Android, Material, or accessibility standard release occurs;
- navigation or system UI behavior changes;
- a source receives a major new edition;
- the skill begins recommending components or metrics that no longer match current SDKs.

Do not silently rely on remembered version-specific details when current official guidance is available.
